DR. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

11th President of India

Introduction

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an exceptional Indian scientist, statesman, and educator who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam made significant contributions to India's space and missile programs.


He played a crucial role in the development of India's ballistic missile and nuclear weapons programs. Dr. Kalam was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pioneering work in ballistic missile technology. He led various scientific projects and served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India from 1992 to 1999.


Apart from his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was widely admired for his humility, integrity, and dedication to inspiring the youth. He was a prolific writer and speaker, encouraging young people to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals. His books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," have inspired millions around the world.


Even after his presidency, Kalam remained active in public life, focusing on education, innovation, and societal development. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of scientific excellence, patriotism, and service to humanity.

Highlights

  • Missile Man of India: Kalam played a pivotal role in developing India's ballistic missile and nuclear weapons programs, earning him the moniker "Missile Man of India."
  • Scientific Leadership: He served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and was instrumental in various scientific projects, contributing significantly to India's defense capabilities.
  • Presidency: Dr. Kalam was the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. He brought a scientific temperament and a focus on youth empowerment to the highest office in the country.
  • Inspiring Youth: Kalam was renowned for his humility, integrity, and dedication to inspiring young people. His speeches and books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," continue to motivate millions worldwide.
  • Public Service: Even after his presidency, Kalam remained active in public life, advocating for education, innovation, and societal development.
